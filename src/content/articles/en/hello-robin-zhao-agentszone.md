## What I'm building (three threads)

### 1. FDE · 90-day field playbook (discipline, not body shop)

I define FDE as **enterprise AI-era partner**, not IT systems engineer. Success is pinned to gates:

- **W4**: scope + KPI + ≤2 integrations, Steering in writing  
- **W8**: demo-ready PoC; human review **must be clicked through** in the demo  
- **W12**: bundle + Runbook + customer IT can recite the main path  

We don't sign off on revenue or funding — those are business outcomes. I sign off on **auditable, continuable, handoff-ready** delivery.

### 2. Ontology-Robin · semantic chains

Excel → graph → four-step Agent reasoning. Object–relation–action–evidence, with full W8 scripts for retail finance and similar domains. Same family as Harness golden sets: **don't let Agents pass silently**.

### 3. IP evaluation & transfer (Scenario D)

University/park-style loops: **navigation → assessment → patent library → listing/intent → tech transfer**. Engineering must encode broker Deal/HALT gates — when `licenseTrimmable=no`, the UI blocks; don't punt to legal after the fact.

## How I relate to Ma Chi's FDE piece

I agree with much of the critique: “platform + embedded engineers” without a product is selling person-days; Palantir isn't a blind template.

I still work on the front line because many Chinese customers don't need the Nth Copilot entry. They need:

1. **Someone to freeze scope** (or Agents only accelerate scope creep)  
2. **Someone to write acceptance as scripts** (or W12 is code without sign-off)  
3. **Someone willing to say “human review here” in the demo** (or compliance lives in slides and incidents live in prod)

If that's FDE, I'll take the label. If not, suggest a better Chinese name — I care about **W12 sign-off**, not the badge.
